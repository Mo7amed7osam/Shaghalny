# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Students / freelancers:** Early-career university students in MENA (primarily Egypt) using the platform on a laptop or phone, daytime, on campus or at home. Primary task: discover jobs, submit proposals, complete skill interviews, manage contracts.

**Clients / job posters:** Small teams and individual hiring managers reviewing applicants on desktop, office or home office, typically during work hours. Primary task: post jobs, evaluate proposals, accept and fund contracts.

**Admins / reviewers:** Internal operators reviewing AI interview output, managing skill library, approving payments. Desktop-heavy workflow.

## Product Purpose

Shaghalny is a student-focused freelancing marketplace that reduces the trust gap between early-career freelancers and clients. It offers structured job posting, proposal workflows, AI-guided video skill verification (Gravis), AI-driven cover letter enhancement, AI career roadmaps, real-time events and notifications, contracts with escrow, and wallet/payout management. It is also available as a Progressive Web App (PWA) with offline capabilities. Success means: a student can prove skills, plan their career, and earn on the platform; a client can hire verified student talent with less manual screening.

## Brand Personality

Trusted, clear, student-forward. Three words: structured, credible, approachable.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with hero-metric templates (big number + gradient accent)
- Consumer-cute or student-playful aesthetics (bright gradients, bubbly type)
- Dark-mode-by-default fintech/crypto aesthetics
- Cluttered marketplace UIs that bury actions under menus

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** Students are here to find work and get paid; clients are here to hire. Every screen should immediately surface the primary action.
2. **Trust is earned by clarity.** Verification status, proposal state, contract progress, and payment status must always be visible and unambiguous.
3. **Density where it helps, space where it matters.** Data tables can be dense. Form pages and dashboards need breathing room.
4. **Consistent vocabulary across roles.** Same button shape, same form controls, same badge semantics. A student and a client should feel like they are using the same product.
5. **Destructive actions need friction.** Delete, decline, and release-escrow flows must signal consequence and require intentional confirmation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Semantic HTML, labeled inputs, visible focus rings, sufficient contrast. Support for reduced-motion. Mobile-first responsive layout.
